I think just about everyone is familiar with what is happening in Ukraine at this point.

Although, some might not be aware of just how much money and business being pulled out of Russia as we speak.

To name a few companies you might be familiar with; Visa, Mastercard, Netflix, Samsung, TikTok, Nike, BP, GM, Ford, Toyota, Volkswagen, Disney, Spotify, Apple, Microsoft, McDonalds, Pepsi, and even Coca-Cola! Freaking Coca-Cola!

We are not just talking about slowing down, I mean all out we are done, doors closed, go home kind of done. Not just for moral reasons, but also to avoid consumer backlash for doing business with Russia at all.

With all this noise I'm sure those who follow the market closely also noticed the sharp drop in prices. While it could have been a good ‘buy the dip’ opportunity, I think the biggest fear right now is from rising oil prices and potential recession from inflation, or worse. Sadly I cannot say I'm not in that group at least a little bit.

However, a warning to the wise, don’t go over the edge with fear, let things unfold. Don’t emotionally change your portfolio on a whim just because of something you read on the news.

A steadfast mindset, strong strategy, and a reasonable time horizon wins 9 times out of 10.
