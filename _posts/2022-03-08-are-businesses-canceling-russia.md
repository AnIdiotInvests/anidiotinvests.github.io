I think just about everyone is familiar with what is happening in Ukraine at this point. So I won’t bore you with the details.

Although, some might not be aware of just how much money and business is being pulled out of Russia as we speak.

To name a few companies you might be familiar with; Visa, Mastercard, Netflix, Samsung, TikTok, Nike, BP, General Motors, Ford, Toyota, Volkswagen, Disney, Spotify, Apple, Microsoft, McDonald's, Pepsi, and even Coca-Cola!

Freaking Coca-Cola!

We are not just talking about slowing down business, I mean all out we are done here, doors closed, go home kind of done. Not just for moral reasons and concerns, but also to avoid consumer backlash for doing business with Russia at all.

## What Does This Mean?

I think it’s obvious when you pile the effects of sanctions on top of this, the effects would be devastating to any economy. Just take a look at the Russian Ruble for reference. It’s been in a bit of a decline for a while but has had roughly a -50% drop in a short period.

{% include image-caption.html image="ruble-graph.png" caption="Screenshot of google Ruble to USD conversion graph" %}

With all this noise I'm sure those who follow the stock market closely also noticed the sharp drop in prices. While it could have been a good ‘buy the dip’ opportunity, I think the biggest fear right now is from rising oil prices and potential recession from inflation, or worse.

Sadly, I cannot claim I am a true contrarian here. I do get some chills down my spine when I turn on the news. I'm still hopeful though and holding off on being bearish.

## Final Thoughts

However, a warning to the wise, don’t go over the edge with fear. Instead, try to let major events unfold without overthinking them. Don’t emotionally change your portfolio and investment strategy on a whim just because of something you read on the news.

Panic selling to avoid possible losses is not an adaptive maneuver, it's just expensive fear.

A well-thought-out strategy, steadfast mindset, and a reasonable time horizon win nine times out of ten.

But if you are feeling frisky, there may be times when you can adapt to changing business or political environments to snag assets at a discount.

(Not saying I am capable of this type of degeneracy though). I tend stick to my usual stock picking Wall Street donations instead to loose my money.
