As I have slowly progressed my investing knowledge over the years several online and mostly free tools that provide great value and time saving have come to my attention. So I thought it would be a good idea to make a quick write up on my favorites thus far. Also, I am not affiliated with any of these sites and tools, but I do use some of them almost daily.

As a disclaimer, if you have not heard about any of these options or had bad experiences, just know I am not claiming they are the best ever crated. They are the best I have found, and the order I have them listed doesn't really matter either.

## PortfoliosLab.com
- Link: https://portfolioslab.com/

Let me start by saying, this site kicks serious @$$ for newer investors who may not yet know exactly what they are looking for yet (AKA me) and want to familiarize themselves with some possibly lesser known aspects for investing in a visual way. But it is also great for more experienced investors who want an easy place to start backtesting their portfolio ideas. More specifically, its biggest strength is comparing different types of volatility and drawdown.

## PortfolioVisualizer.com
- Link: https://www.portfoliovisualizer.com/

Extremely similar to PortfoliosLab.com, but has some unique features.

## SeekingAlpha.com
- Link: https://seekingalpha.com/

SeekingAlpha is more oriented toward its fantastic content and articles related to investing and finance. However, it also features some useful tools built into the site.

## TradingView.com
- Link: https://www.tradingview.com/

If you have not used TradingView yet, you are missing out. It is a great way to not only screen different assets and crypto, but it is super nice to have a live chart feed for free without having to be logged into your brokerage account. This tool is hard to beat, even on some paid platforms.

## Etfrc.com
- Link: https://www.etfrc.com/

Have you ever wondered if you might be buying too much of a specific sector or specific set of companies while trying to diversify with different ETFs? Well, etfrc is the place to be. I hate that I cannot add more than two ETFs at a time, but this has saved me a lot of trouble reading through prospectus PDFs junk on Vanguard and Fidelity screener to compare my ETF selections and allocations.

## Etfdb.com
- Link: https://etfdb.com/

Has a very comprehensive selection of data and has access to many of the newer product that may not be listed elsewhere. Has some great comparison and search tools as well, may not be as good as previous options though.

### Honorable Mentions
- Etf.com - Similar to etfdb, but a tad bit more clunky in my opinion.
- Marketwatch.com - I like the virtual trading games to learn and track your favorite investments.
- Zacks.com - Really good screener, but UI is a bit weird for me.
