I want to take a moment to talk about my extremely fluffy cat.

This is no ordinary cat, this is Mocha the cat. As in Mocha Frappuccino the cat.

My girlfriend and I argue continuously about the last name of this cat, but it's definitely my name that will end up on the adoption certificate.

Even now she just told me my days are numbered as I write this.

I have a love-hate relationship with this cat. Which obviously tilts toward love. Still, I can't help but be bitter when she scratches my couch and walls. On the surface this seems like the polar opposite of a good investment monetarily speaking.

But what if I told you a cat is actually an investment. Not just for the cuteness, but for the emotional support!

If you don’t understand and you don’t like mammals, get a lizard or an active fish or something, that's ok too.

## How My Cat Helped Me

I had fallen a bit down on myself a few weeks ago, I can't help but second guess every donation I make to Wall Street in failed attempts to make more moola. So I found myself lying fully clothed, face down on my bed for a few hours. I started to have some rather challenging thoughts about my future plans.

(Even though I invest for the long-run, I do make a few short term plays here and there, but this day hit my retirement account pretty hard too.)

And there she was to the rescue. She layed down, right on my back and started purring. She had never done anything like this in the six months I had adopted her.

A cat human sandwich comes to mind as a visual.

Obviously, I had to pet her for the next 20 minutes, but the point is she cheered me up.

## Final Thoughts

Cats are just fancy ferrets… not really.

There is something about cats and dogs, they just seem to know when you feel like shit. Or when you make a bad financial decision. Or when you get an insurance bill you forget that eats half your paycheck the day it comes in. Then sadness set in etcetera. 

I am not at all saying a pet is one hundred percent guaranteed to make you happy, but it could definitely help. Especially in your lowest moments it's nice to have something there that has your back, or in my case, sits on it.
