Does anyone remember when we were yielding more than treasury bonds by simply putting our money in high yield savings accounts?

God, I miss that so much.

I know there are exceptions, but for the most part, everybody's sitting around half a percent or less right now. I have used Ally bank for several years, and it used to yield 2%+ a year once upon a time.

I complain about going back in time a lot, and this is no exception.

I just want free money lol.

## Fed Rates VS My Rates?

More than a few sources have claimed that as the government raises its rates, many of the popular online savings banks will raise their saving interest rates in response.

I know this is not a guarantee, but for me, this would be fantastic news.

Earning only half a percent of my money has caused rapid convulsions, heart attacks, and explosive gas.

Also, the fear of investing more of my savings to keep up with inflation when I would rather use it as an emergency fund is also impacting my overall mental health.

## When Will Account Rates Go Back Up?

The short answer is no one really knows.

Even if federal rates do go up enough to have an effect, it could take eons for the difference in earning power to trickle through the economy. Let alone invoke enough change for banks to raise their savings interest rates to match pre 2020 levels.

Many of these banks only raised their rates to attract customers, but I am a bit skeptical they will ever go back up that high at all.

At least for a vast number of banks that is.

Part of me wants to believe that rates will go back to the way they were, and inflation will get curbed in a reasonable amount of time. But I also want to have millions of dollars in my bank, the ability to fly, and obtaining immortality would be nice icing on the cake.

If I had to make a guess right now though, I would say the middle of 2023 would be the soonest or the end of 2026 by the latest.

But that's only based on some [historical rate changes and evidence](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/FEDFUNDS). Especially if you go back far enough to look at how the Fed has handled inflation in the past.

The problem is the current circumstances, like many things in life, whether we choose to believe it or not, are extremely dynamic and are different from the past.

While we're not necessarily experiencing hyperinflation on paper, some may argue that the scenario in which inflation is occurring is crazy enough to invoke much higher rates in the future in a much shorter time frame than expected.

Fortunately, the Fed (so far) has not reacted with any rug pulls or extreme rate hikes which may have caused panic for investors.

## Final Thoughts

My primary hobbies nowadays are junk food from WholeFoods, craft beer, Webtoons, and Markiplier videos on YouTube super late at night. It would be nice if my bank was paying for my hobbies when interest rates go back up to pre-COVID levels, but I'm not holding my breath.

Even though I would not be surprised if rates did go back up, I also wouldn't be surprised if it took five years for the changes to have enough effect that our savings accounts change in accordance.

If you are looking for an alternative, you might try [I-Bonds](https://www.treasurydirect.gov/indiv/research/indepth/ibonds/res_ibonds.htm) (NOT AFFILIATED). But if inflation goes down, then you have cash stuck in a stupid bond.

That would put your finances in a real bond with bonds… bummer.
