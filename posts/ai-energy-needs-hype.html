What If AI Energy Stocks Are Not Fully Priced In Yet?

Date: 21 June 2025 | Author: Josh The Destroyer

AI Is Hungry For Energy

Overview

  • Trying to find valuable articles on topics other than AI and tech is a bit annoying.
  • Will the energy be needed to continue training AI grow? Or will AI energy needs plateau?
  • What is the possibility of this energy need not being already fully priced?

Bombarded By AI Articles

Typically, I don't try to beat the overall market on well-known trends. Deep down, I know I'm competing with large entities and people much smarter than I am for limited, ever-changing information that they can get much more quickly than any mainstream sources.

That said, however.

As I continue researching, many sources continue to discuss tech stocks (AI-focused, obviously) and the needs of AI. (Many of which are written by AIs ironically.)

More specifically, the massive data centers and the insane amount of energy needed to train and then run the AI.

Not to mention all the news about possible nuclear reactor deals and massive new data centers in the works…

In Fact, depending on your social platform, it has become a bit cliche to invest in data centers and nuclear energy-related equities. Even self-storage and warehouse REITs may have already had their time in the sun as well.

Yet this hasn't stopped me from being interested in it.

The Focus

My focus is mostly on the energy needs for training and the idea that power usage is not linear. It is exponential, and companies seem to be ok with footing the bill so far.

The question I can’t find much backing for is, once the AI is “trained” to the point it's considered “profitable”, will there still be a need for the vast amounts exponentially growing energy?

This also assumes it will be or remain profitable.

Another way to put it, will AI always need more and more energy or will it eventually plateau?

While I personally don't have enough confidence it tilt my portfolio in this direction artificially, if it doesn’t then I don’t see an end to the demand.
