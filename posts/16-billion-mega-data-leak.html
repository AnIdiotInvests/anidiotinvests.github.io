My Data Is On the Dark Web... Again?

Date: 21 June 2025 | Author: Josh The Destroyer

A breaking safe to represent the data breach/leak

Overview

(This event is still unfolding and will be updated as more accurate info is released)

  • Possibly the largest data breach ever recorded of roughly 16 billion login credentials.
  • The data most likely comes from various infostealers.
  • The data is recent, not just a collection of data from older data breaches.

A Big Leak

16 Billion.

Not million. Billion. That could be 2 accounts for every person alive, but its MORE compliated that that.

It is believed this was not a targeted attack on individual companies like Google or Facebook.

Instead, this may be more of a large-scale aggregation of stolen user data collected over time through a type of malware known as "infostealers."

While the total records does tally a whopping 16 billion, its composed of multiple datasets with 'possible' overalp. Emphasis on possible, still searching for credible source.

The most alarming part is that this isn't just old data compiled from past breaches, it appears to be collected recently, at least according to multiple cybersecurity sources.

As with many other data breaches, the stolen data can end up on the dark web. So its a good idea to chagne your passwords as quickly as possible.

Another Problem

Why does it feel like no one cares?

I think people do care, but there is a sense of fatigue and futility.

No one believes anything can be done.

So far, most markets have barely reacted.
